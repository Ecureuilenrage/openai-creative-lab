Tarot

From stained-glass experiments to a minimalist deck

Interactive AI application

The Tarot project is an interactive card-reading web application, available at tarot.exoniq.io. The user asks a question, chooses a deck (stained-glass or minimalist) and a spread (1 or 3 cards), then discovers their cards through a dealing and flipping animation. Each reading is interpreted by AI, accompanied by an image generated in the chosen deck's style.

Visual Exploration

Process frame

This chapter traces the evolution of the Tarot project as a creative process — rather than a simple gallery of finished cards. Each section below corresponds to a key stage of that journey.

  • Follow the project's evolution rather than a simple gallery of cards.
  • Explain that the early stained-glass video attempts were kept as still images inside the project, but not as the final direction for the clips.
  • Highlight the minimalist deck as a choice for readability, coherence, and efficiency in the final experience.

02

Style Evolution

Style Evolution

The transition from ornate stained-glass styles to a minimalist deck was driven by three priorities: readability, coherence, and efficiency. Early experiments produced visually rich but heavy cards that competed with each other — the final direction embraces restraint so the deck works as a unified system.

03

Bulk Generation

Bulk Generation

Controlled variation was essential to producing the 22 major arcana. The bulk generation pipeline combined prompt engineering with systematic iteration — each pass refining framing, coherence, and readability across the entire set.

04

Interactive App

Interactive App

The app went through several versions before reaching its current form. The user journey: a text box to ask a question (with three pre-written suggestions), a configuration screen to choose the deck (stained-glass or minimalist) and card count (1 or 3), an animation where the deck pulses then deals cards face down, a click to flip each card as a video (upright or reversed), and finally a results page with the AI reading, a synthesis, and an image generated based on the drawn cards and deck style.

05

Final Experience

Final experience

From stained-glass experiments to a minimalist 22 major arcana deck, from VS Code prototypes to a live ritual-like draw experience — the Tarot project traces a complete creative journey through AI-assisted design and development.
